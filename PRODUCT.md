# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary audience: recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Lucas for a front-end software engineering role. They arrive with limited time, scanning for fit, seniority, and proof before deciding whether to shortlist.

## Product Purpose

This site exists to prove Lucas is a strong front-end engineer with real product impact — not a tech-list résumé. Success means a recruiter shortlists him for an interview based on what they see.

## Positioning

The full front-end stack in one person: React and React Native, interface design in Figma, plus mentoring and leadership.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Download CV, placed inside the About me card.
- Secondary fallback: ask the site chat about Lucas, or open other cards to see more detail.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: Confident front-end hire — product impact plus the full front-end stack.
- Belief ladder: (1) clear who Lucas is → (2) recent role and wins look relevant → (3) skills match the req → (4) download the CV.
- Proof on hand: existing CV at `public/downloads/lucasviga-frontend-se.pdf`, experience achievements on the experience surface, plus education and skills data in `src/data/`.

## Brand Personality

**Confident · Clear · Crafted.** The first emotion on landing should be confidence — “this person knows what they’re doing.” Voice is direct and professional without coldness; the interface itself should feel like evidence of taste.

Visual direction for the redesign (strategic, not token-level): a soft light bento dashboard inspired by the attached glass-card reference — chat-style input as the hero (“talk with the website” about skills, education, and everything about Lucas), then five equal-style cards: About me, Experience, Education, Skills, and Follow me (Twitter, Medium, LinkedIn, Figma, GitHub). Card styling should match the reference’s soft surfaces, large radii, and airy spacing.

## Anti-references

- Dark “dev portfolio” clichés: neon purple, terminal vibes, dense tech-stack walls.
- Generic Bootstrap / template résumés.

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over inventory** — Prefer outcomes, role clarity, and scannable evidence over long tool lists.
2. **Ask, then explore** — The chat input invites curiosity; the cards deliver structured proof without forcing a linear scroll.
3. **One confident first impression** — Hierarchy and copy should make “who / what / why hire” obvious in seconds.
4. **Craft is the credential** — Layout, type, and interaction quality should reinforce hireability as much as the content does.
5. **Equal card grammar** — The five cards share one visual system so recruiters read content, not competing chrome.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Keyboard-friendly navigation and interaction for the chat input, cards, and primary CTA. No formal WCAG target beyond that.
